Adoption requirements
Age requirement: Adopters are usually required to be at least 18 years old.
Identity and residence proof: Provide a photo ID with your name and current address. If you are renting, provide a written proof of the landlord's consent to keep pets.
Visa requirements: In Australia, if the adopter is not an Australian citizen, he/she needs to provide his/her own visa, and the visa will be rejected if the visa is less than one year.
Adoption Process
Choose your pet: Visit the RSPCA's state-specific website or download the app to browse adoptable animal profiles and select your ideal companion.
To apply, click the "Apply to adopt" button on the pet page and fill out the adoption application form, including your family situation, pet experience, and living environment.
Review and communication: The staff will contact the applicant within 1-3 working days to preliminarily understand his/her living situation, pet experience, daily routine, etc., to ensure the applicant and pet match.Meeting: Applicants visit the adoption center to meet and interact with pets offline to see if they are suitable for each other.Cooling-off period: Some RSPCA offices may offer a 24-hour cooling-off period to allow adopters to ensure they have made the right decision.
Home visit: Before adoption, the RSPCA may arrange a staff home visit to see if the living environment is suitable for the pet and to meet all family members and existing pets.
Adoption trial period: Some states offer trial periods. For example, the RSPCA NSW provides a 'Pet Trial Program' to allow adopters and pets to adjust to each other.
Official adoption: After the probation period, if both parties are satisfied, the adopter will pay the adoption fee and complete the relevant paperwork, and then the pet can be officially adopted.Adoption fees and included services
Cost: The adoption fee for cats is usually 150-250 Australian dollars, and for dogs 200-500 Australian dollars, depending on factors such as breed and age.
Service content: The cost includes vaccination, insect control, sterilization, chip implantation and other services.
Adoption responsibilities and obligationsProvide proper care: Adopters need to promise to provide proper diet, shelter, medical care, etc., including regular health check-ups and vaccinations for pets.
Comply with regulations: All adopted cats and dogs must be neutered and microchipped, and other animals should be handled according to their species 'characteristics. Adopters must follow the RSPCA's guidelines and local animal protection laws
Follow-up after adoption: The RSPCA may conduct a follow-up visit within 8-12 weeks to ensure the pet is doing well in the new home and the adopter continues to fulfill their care responsibilities.
